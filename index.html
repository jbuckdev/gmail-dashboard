      Help & Troubleshooting

      How This Works

      This page reads your emails directly from Gmail, sorts them by importance, and writes a summary of what people told you. Here is what happens behind the scenes:

      • 1. You sign in with Google, which gives this page temporary, read-only access to your email. It cannot send, move, or delete anything.
      • 2. Your emails are read right here in your browser. Obvious noise (newsletters, promotions, auto-replies) is filtered out instantly using built-in rules.
      • 3. The remaining emails are sent to Claude (by Anthropic) for analysis. Claude reads the content and decides what is important, assigns topics, and writes your briefing.
      • 4. When you close this tab, everything is forgotten. Nothing is saved to any server or database.

      Common Issues

      "Written briefing unavailable"

      This usually means one of these things:

      • ● Not enough important emails — If your inbox is mostly newsletters and notifications, there is not enough substance for a written summary. This is normal during quiet periods.
      • ● Claude key issue — Your key may be expired or invalid. Go to Preferences and paste a new one. You can generate a key at console.anthropic.com.
      • ● No credits — Your Claude account may need a payment method or credits added. Check your balance at console.anthropic.com/billing. Normal usage costs less than $1 per month.

      "Google sign-in expired"

      Google sign-in sessions last about one hour. If you leave the page open for a while and then refresh, you may need to sign in again. Just reload the page and click "Sign in with Google." Your preferences and Claude key are saved — you will not need to set those up again.

      Emails are in the wrong category

      The sorting is not perfect. If important people are being buried, add them to My Important Contacts in Preferences. If a sender keeps showing up that you do not care about, add them to Senders I Never Need to See. These lists are the most powerful way to improve your results.

      Dashboard is loading slowly

      The monthly view reads more emails and takes longer. Try the daily or weekly view for faster results. A slow internet connection can also cause delays — each email requires a quick call to Gmail and to Claude.

      Text is too small

      Go to Preferences and use the Text Size buttons to switch between Normal, Large, and Extra Large.

      Your Privacy

      Your emails are your business. Here is exactly where your data goes:

      • Your browser — All email data lives here temporarily while the page is open. When you close the tab, it is gone.
      • Google — Your emails are already stored by Google. This page reads them through the same connection Gmail uses. Nothing new is stored.
      • Anthropic (Claude) — Email content from real people (not newsletters) is sent to Claude for analysis. Anthropic may retain this data for up to 30 days for safety monitoring, then permanently deletes it. It is never used to train AI.
      • This website — The page itself is hosted on GitHub. GitHub never sees your email data — it only serves the page code.
      • Private Mode — If you switch to Private Mode in Preferences, nothing leaves your browser at all. No email data is sent to Claude. Sorting is less precise but fully private.

      Quick Reference

      • Needs Your Reply — Someone is waiting for you to respond, approve, or decide something.
      • Worth Reading — Important updates, reports, or information. No action needed, but you should know about it.
      • FYI — Newsletters or notifications that might be mildly interesting. Quick glance at most.
      • Safe to Skip — Promotions, social notifications, auto-replies, and other noise. You are not missing anything.
      • Email Landscape — The bubble map on the right shows topics in your inbox. Bigger bubble means more emails about that topic. Click any bubble to explore.
      • Briefing — The written summary below "Good morning" tells you what people said in their emails, in plain English. Click any name to open that email in Gmail.
